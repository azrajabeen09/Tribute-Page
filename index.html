About Sania Mirza

Sania Mirza is one of India's most famous tennis players and a global icon in women's tennis. She was born on November 15, 1986 in Mumbai and raised in Hyderabad. She began playing tennis at a very young age and quickly became one of the most promising players in India.

Sania made history by becoming the first Indian woman to win a WTA singles title and later dominated the doubles circuit. Her powerful groundstrokes, aggressive playing style, and determination made her one of the most recognizable faces of Indian sports.

Career Journey

Early Career

Sania Mirza started playing tennis professionally in the early 2000s. She quickly gained attention for her powerful forehand and fearless attitude on the court.

2005 Breakthrough

In 2005, she became the first Indian woman to win a WTA singles title at the Hyderabad Open. This victory made her a national icon.

Doubles Dominance

After focusing more on doubles tennis, Sania achieved massive success with several Grand Slam titles and became the World No.1 in women's doubles.

Grand Slam Success

She won multiple Grand Slam titles in doubles and mixed doubles including Australian Open, Wimbledon, and US Open.

Retirement

Sania Mirza officially retired from professional tennis in 2023, leaving behind an incredible legacy in Indian sports.

Major Achievements

World No.1

Sania Mirza became the World No.1 in Women's Doubles in 2015.

Grand Slam Titles

She won 6 Grand Slam titles across doubles and mixed doubles competitions.

Padma Bhushan

She was awarded the Padma Bhushan, one of India's highest civilian honors.

Olympics Representation

Sania represented India at multiple Olympic Games.

Arjuna Award

She received the Arjuna Award for her outstanding achievements in tennis.

Role Model

Sania inspired millions of girls in India to pursue sports professionally.
